Camera Modes

There are a few basic camera modes, they are: Full auto, Creative auto, Aperture priority, Shutter priority, and Manual. Below is a definition of each along with a table with a general idea of what camera types will have which settings.

Full auto: this just means that your camera takes care of all the technical stuff so you donâ€™t have to and can just start shooting.

Creative auto: this is like full auto, but in creative auto you can have control over certain things. Such as, depth of field and motion blur.

Aperture priority: this lets you set the aperture of your camera to what you want and the camera will automatically set the shutter speed.

Shutter priority: this is like aperture priority except the other way around.

Manual: this is the exact opposite of full auto. You have full control over your settings and all the camera does is capture the picture.

Note: this is just a general list. See your camera's manual to see what settings it has
