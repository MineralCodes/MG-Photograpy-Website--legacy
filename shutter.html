Shutter Speed

Shutter speed controls how long and how much light is allowed to get to your digital sensor. The longer the exposure the more light is allowed to get to your sensor and the brighter the picture will be. Shutter speed is measured in stops, each stop either cuts the amount of light in half or doubles it. Stops range from 1/4000 of a second to 30 seconds, and then there’s a bulb setting which will keep the shutter open as long as the shutter button is pressed.

Shutter speed is also used to control motion blur, the longer the exposure the more moving objects will blur. A fast speed such as 1/1000 of a second will freeze just about any motion, where a speed of 1/80 of a second will allow some moving objects to blur. If you shutter speed gets too slow you will start to see blurring from camera shake. A good rule of thumb is 1/60 of a second is the slowest you can handhold your camera, anything slowe than that and you’re going to need a tripod.
